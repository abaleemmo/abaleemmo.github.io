MASIHA Mobile Food Pantry Volunteer Director

caption filler text 2

caption filler text 3

Since August of 2021 I have served as a volunteer with the Mobile Food Pantry, a food pantry that operates in the greater Evansville Metropolitan Area under the umbrella of the Muslim Association of Southern Indiana for Health Advancement (MASIHA). My role as a volunteer has been to section donated and purchased food into bags for distribution on a weekly basis. Overtime, my role evolved into that of a volunteer director, which required me to coordinate between all of the volunteers working in the food pantry and direct them accordingly. This meant that I was dealing with people coordinating their efforts, dealing with food and ensuring bags were made with ample nutritious value and adhered to a specific demographic (ex. homeless demographics were given pre-cooked food that simply needed to be microwaved whereas the less fortunate with homes were given canned foods to be prepared). Simultaneously dealing with all of these moving pieces has given me a real eye for detail and has allowed me to think at a macroscopic level and direct large groups of people efficiently.
